# Brand Review Workflow

## Purpose

Document how brand review may occur across the project lifecycle.

## Current Decisions

- Working direction: brand checks should occur during intake, review, and closeout.
- Working direction: brand governance should support consistency without creating unnecessary bottlenecks.

## Request Intake

- Check whether the request uses approved logos, templates, copy, CTA language, or photography direction.
- Identify ministry sub-brand needs.
- Flag requests that need brand review before production begins.

## Project Setup

- Attach relevant brand guidelines, templates, and approved assets.
- Confirm audience, desired feeling, desired action, and ministry context.
- Identify stakeholders responsible for brand or ministry approval.

## Design Review

- Review layout, copy, imagery, typography, logo use, color, CTA, and ministry fit.
- Record requested changes and approval decisions.
- Distinguish brand feedback from content or ministry preference feedback.

## Stakeholder Approval

- Confirm stakeholder approval after brand review issues are resolved.
- Track who approved what and when.
- Capture exceptions or special-case decisions.

## Final Sign-Off

- Verify final deliverables match approved brand direction.
- Confirm final files are ready for publication, production, or delivery.
- Mark approved versions clearly.

## Closeout/Archive

- Preserve final brand-related assets.
- Add reusable templates or assets to Brand Center or Media Library.
- Record where assets were used.
- Capture lessons learned for future brand guidance.

## Open Questions

- Which projects require formal brand review?
- Who can approve brand exceptions?
- Should ministry sub-brands have separate approval rules?

# Pain Points

## Purpose

Document the expanded operational problems ForWorship Creative is intended to address for church communications teams.

## Current Decisions

- Working direction: the product should solve communications operations problems, not generic project tracking alone.
- Working direction: the platform should reduce administrative coordination, improve request quality, preserve creative memory, and support brand consistency.
- Working decision: the product must not add more overhead than it removes.
- Working decision: the platform should create calm, not complexity.

## Communications Work Management

### Problem

Church communications teams may manage 20+ projects across 10+ departments, with each department often assuming its project is the only priority.

### Why It Matters

Communications teams are crushed by coordination, status updates, follow-up, prioritization, and project traffic management.

### Product Response

Centralize projects, requests, tasks, status, timelines, approvals, messages, and files in one operational workspace.

### MVP Relevance

High. The MVP should make active work easier to see, prioritize, and move forward.

## Request Quality and Ministry Preparedness

### Problem

Departments often submit incomplete or unclear requests.

### Why It Matters

Unclear requests create rework, delays, missed expectations, and extra meetings before production can begin.

### Product Response

Use guided request intake to help departments think through purpose, audience, timing, CTA, stakeholders, budget, assets, and approval needs.

### MVP Relevance

High. Structured intake is a core MVP priority.

## Communication Timelines

### Problem

Departments do not always know what is happening when. Timelines get delayed, rushed, or miscommunicated.

### Why It Matters

Timeline confusion creates last-minute work, missed publication windows, and stakeholder frustration.

### Product Response

Track production deadlines, communication timelines, publication dates, review windows, and visibility for all involved parties.

### MVP Relevance

High. Basic timeline tracking should be included in MVP planning.

## Stakeholders, Sign-Offs, and Budgets

### Problem

Projects need clear stakeholder tracking, approval/sign-off workflows, budget visibility, cost tracking, and decision history.

### Why It Matters

Without clear ownership and sign-off, teams lose time resolving who approved what, what changed, and why.

### Product Response

Track stakeholders, approvals, sign-offs, budgets, costs, decision history, and project activity.

### MVP Relevance

High. MVP should include basic approvals and budget/cost tracking.

## Department Utilization Reporting

### Problem

Churches often lack visibility into how much communications work each department uses.

### Why It Matters

Annual planning, staffing, ministry priorities, and budget conversations are harder without clear work volume and time data.

### Product Response

Track time, budget, volume, type of work, and department usage for reporting.

### MVP Relevance

Medium to high. MVP should capture enough structured data to support basic department utilization reporting.

## Self-Service Project Visibility

### Problem

Stakeholders and departments frequently ask Communications for status updates.

### Why It Matters

Repeated status requests interrupt focused work and increase administrative load.

### Product Response

Provide review-aware project status, timelines, pending approvals, and blockers to departments, reviewers, and External Reviewers where scope allows.

### MVP Relevance

High. Review visibility should be considered part of the core value.

## Media and Asset Chaos

### Problem

Photos, graphics, videos, design files, and media assets come from multiple sources and are difficult to track.

### Why It Matters

Teams lose time finding files, confirming final versions, recreating work, and determining usage rights or context.

### Product Response

Provide a media library with metadata, project relationships, asset status, usage tracking, versions, notes, people in image, and reusable context.

### MVP Relevance

High. Media library should connect to projects in MVP planning.

## Project Closeout and Institutional Memory

### Problem

Projects are often marked done without a structured archive of source files, exports, vendor files, notes, approvals, and final deliverables.

### Why It Matters

Future teams cannot easily find, reuse, audit, or understand past creative work.

### Product Response

Add a closeout workflow that verifies deliverables, archives files, records metadata, tags reusable assets, captures lessons learned, and moves projects to Archived.

### MVP Relevance

Medium to high. A basic closeout workflow should be included, with advanced automation deferred.

## Tool-Specific Creative File Handling

### Problem

InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop, Figma, Canva, video tools, and photography workflows each have different archive needs.

### Why It Matters

Missing fonts, linked files, access permissions, exports, or source files can make past work unusable.

### Product Response

Define tool-specific archive checklists and future integration possibilities.

### MVP Relevance

Medium. MVP can document and checklist requirements without deep platform integrations.

## Brand Communication and Management

### Problem

Brand management is often scattered across files, opinions, folders, templates, and informal review habits.

### Why It Matters

Churches need consistent communication across ministries, campuses, campaigns, print, web, social, video, and signage.

### Product Response

Create a Brand Center for approved assets, messaging standards, templates, ministry sub-brand guidance, voice and tone, photography guidelines, and governance workflows.

### MVP Relevance

Medium to high. Basic brand guidance and approved assets may be MVP; advanced governance may be post-MVP.

## Software Overhead Risk

### Problem

Operational software can become another system that requires more management than the work it replaces.

### Why It Matters

Church communications teams already have too much coordination work. A tool that adds unnecessary fields, decisions, notifications, or setup burden will fail the core product promise.

### Product Response

Use calm software principles: opinionated defaults, guided intake, simple templates, clear status, limited customization, self-service visibility, and workflows that help and get out of the way.

### MVP Relevance

High. MVP should prove that the platform reduces administrative load before adding advanced features.

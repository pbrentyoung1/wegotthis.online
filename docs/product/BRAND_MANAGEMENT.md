# Brand Management

## Purpose

Document brand communication and management as a major product area for wegotthis.online.

## Current Decisions

- Working direction: brand management is part of church communications operations.
- Working direction: Brand Center should connect approved assets, guidelines, templates, request intake, project review, closeout, and the Media Library.
- Working direction: brand management should also protect the warm, conversational, human-centered feel of the product and communications experience.

## Brand Center

- Approved logos
- Color palettes
- Typography
- Voice and tone
- Approved copy blocks
- CTA language
- Conversational UX and copy guidance
- Photography style
- Social media standards
- Video/motion standards
- Ministry sub-brand guidance
- Templates
- Brand governance

## Request Intake Brand Checks

- Help requesters identify whether existing templates, logos, copy blocks, or approved assets already apply.
- Prompt requesters to describe audience, desired feeling, desired action, and ministry context.
- Flag requests that may need brand review.

## Project Review Brand Checks

- Support brand review during design, copy, social, print, signage, and video work.
- Clarify who can approve brand exceptions.
- Preserve decision history when brand direction changes.

## Project Closeout Brand Preservation

- Save approved brand-related deliverables.
- Connect reusable assets to the Media Library.
- Record where brand assets were used.
- Preserve templates, final exports, and source files where appropriate.

## Relationship to Media Library

- Brand assets should be discoverable through the Media Library.
- Media Library records should distinguish approved brand assets from project-specific files.
- Reusable assets should carry context, usage notes, status, version, and ownership details.

## Open Questions

- Is Brand Center part of MVP or early post-MVP?
- Who manages approved brand assets?
- How should ministry sub-brands be represented?
- What brand governance steps are required before final sign-off?

## Notes

- Brand management should support clarity and consistency without turning every project into a bottleneck.
- Brand governance should help the product feel more like a calm teammate and less like a compliance system.

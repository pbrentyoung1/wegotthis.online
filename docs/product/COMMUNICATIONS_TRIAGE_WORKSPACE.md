# Communications Triage Workspace

## Purpose

Define the workspace where Communications reviews incoming requests, clarifies needs, preserves strategic context, and decides what becomes operational work.

The triage workspace should help the team connect ministry goals to communication strategy without turning intake into a heavy planning system.

## Current Decisions

- Requests are separate objects before becoming projects.
- Not every request becomes a project.
- Triage should preserve submitted details, clarification history, requester context, and useful strategic context.
- Accepted requests may convert into projects.
- Converted projects require title, owner, objective, start date, and stop date.
- Campaigns and initiatives are optional strategic context, not required project parents.
- Programs / Content Streams are recurring operational rhythms and should not be modeled as campaigns by default.
- The MVP should not force every request or project into a campaign.
- Triage should use existing Inspinia tables, cards, tabs, messaging/activity feeds, notifications, and form patterns before custom workflow canvases or strategy boards.

## Triage Outcomes

- Needs Clarification
- Accepted
- Deferred
- Rejected
- Converted to Project
- Archived

## Strategic Context During Triage

Triage may capture:

- Initiative
- Campaign
- Program / Content Stream
- Sermon Series
- Season
- Ministry Area
- Topic
- Tags

These fields should explain why the work matters and how it should be found later. They should not replace the project as the operational home after conversion.

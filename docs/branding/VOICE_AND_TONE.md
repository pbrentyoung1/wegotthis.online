# Voice and Tone

## Purpose

Define how the product speaks to users.

## Current Decisions

- Voice and tone should always feel personal.
- The platform should be conversational rather than demanding.
- Tone should feel calm, warm, confident, and encouraging.
- The product should feel supportive, not managerial.
- Avoid corporate SaaS jargon, productivity-bro language, fake cheerfulness, guilt, panic, and unnecessary urgency.
- Use language that makes users feel supported instead of scolded.

## Preferred Direction

- Prefer "What would you like to share?" over "Create announcement."
- Prefer "Who's helping with this?" over "Assign users."
- Prefer "Looks like we still need a date for this." over "Required field missing."
- Prefer "Something didn't go through. Try again and we'll get it sorted." over "Submission failed."

## Open Questions

- What terms should be avoided?
- Which product surfaces should feel most conversational, and which should stay more compact?

## Notes

- Product copy should reduce confusion and avoid insider-only technical language.
- Forms can still exist, but they should feel guided, human, and clear.
- Where possible, the interface should ask good questions instead of presenting cold form labels.
- Conversational interaction should remain lightweight, contextual, and helpful rather than performative.

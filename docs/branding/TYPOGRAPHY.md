# Typography

## Purpose

Record typography choices for product UI and brand materials.

## Current Decisions

- Fraunces is the primary brand typography direction for ForWorship Creative.
- IBM Plex Sans is the primary operational UI font.
- Fraunces should carry headlines, section titles, editorial moments, onboarding headers, campaign and project titles, marketing language, storytelling, important empty states, and small emotional warmth moments.
- IBM Plex Sans should support dense UI, navigation, labels, metadata, tables, dashboards, notifications, and operational text.
- Typography should reinforce that the product feels intelligent, thoughtful, human, calm, and personal.
- Typography emotional system:
  - Fraunces says: "We understand. You're not alone."
  - IBM Plex Sans says: "We can handle this."

## Open Questions

- Are brand and application type scales the same, or does the app use a tighter scale than marketing?
- What exact size and weight rules should govern Fraunces in dense workspace screens?

## Notes

- Typography decisions should account for readability in dashboards and tables.
- Brand warmth should not come at the expense of clarity in operational screens.
- Fraunces expression should feel editorial and human, not decorative for its own sake.
- The typography system should feel like thoughtful software with editorial warmth — not enterprise software, trendy SaaS branding, church bulletin design, or an artisanal ministry journaling app.
- Fraunces is a variable optical size font. Prefer larger optical sizes (`opsz`) at display scale and smaller optical sizes at body scale for best readability.

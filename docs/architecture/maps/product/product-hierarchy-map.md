# Product Structure Map

## Purpose

Define the primary product structure and show how strategic context relates to operational work.

## Core Rules

- Separate Strategic Context from Operational Work.
- Use Projects -> Deliverables -> Tasks as the operational work model.
- Keep campaigns and initiatives optional strategic context.
- Treat Programs / Content Streams as recurring operational rhythms, distinct from campaigns.
- Treat projects as the primary operational container.
- Require projects to have title, owner, objective, start date, and stop date.
- Treat deliverables as the primary production and approval unit.
- Require deliverables to belong to projects.
- Treat tasks as execution units that belong to projects and may optionally belong to deliverables.

## Relationships

- Strategic Context may include initiatives, campaigns, programs/content streams, sermon series, seasons, ministry areas, topics, and tags.
- Projects may associate with strategic context but do not have to.
- Projects should preserve one primary operational home in MVP.
- Projects may contain many deliverables.
- Deliverables must belong to a project and do not stand alone.
- Tasks belong to a project and may optionally belong to a deliverable.
- Requests precede projects and may convert into them.
